Crowd Sourcing & Datamining for Network Quality Analysis

I am working on a source independent framework to define the standard ways of using crowd sourcing data for network quality analysis and optimisation. 


There was no way that big data will not meet field quality measurement. It is finally here and one of the many faces is coming under the general name of crowd-sourcing. 

My first experience with crowd-sourcing for field quality measurement and reporting purposes was with very simple OOKLA® SpeedTest results. It did not take too long after this encounter, that I realized the potential of smart (especially Android) phones. 
Meanwhile, same awareness was growing in many different companies around the world. Since then these companies like Ookla, OpenSignal, Tutela have evolved and many others are joining them. 

However, I still see a gap between those companies who collect the data and mobile network industry. This is because these companies are usually application and/or SDK developers and lack the mobile network engineering point of view while processing their data. For example, even the android developer reference is not very clear on distinguishing different RATs and network quality parameters under each and every one of them. Different implementations of different OSs (again especially Android) are adding to this chaos by not reporting and/or reporting fixed, therefore false, values. This is however not a difficult obstacle, it is basically a standard step of data cleaning in data-mining… and it is fun!  

All in all, like many other data-mining tasks, provided that the critical level of “statistically meaningfulness” is passed, there is an endless potential in extracting valuable information from what smart phones can record. My most recent work is exactly in this area and it excites me more than any task to witness this revolution from the first seat.

    3G Network Optimisation via Geo-Location

    Before data got cheaper and terminals got smarter "optimization via geolocation" was the previous candidate of minimizing the need for drive-tests. I was the principal engineer of a %100 TUBITAK funded R&D project about locating 3G calls via network logs (Ericsson-GPEH, Huawei-PSCHR) and join this information with network optimization parameters. 

    This technology is less interesting now, nevertheless it requires extensive knowledge of 3G PHY layer signalling and parameters.
